Cash Buyers vs Estate Agents in Eastbourne: An Honest Comparison

By Sell My House Fast Eastbourne

Two Routes to Selling Your Eastbourne Property

When you decide to sell your property in Eastbourne, you face a fundamental choice: list with a traditional estate agent and market your home on the open market, or sell directly to a cash buyer for a faster, more certain outcome. Both routes have genuine advantages and drawbacks, and the right choice depends entirely on your circumstances, priorities, and the nature of your property.

This guide provides an honest comparison using real figures relevant to the Eastbourne market. We are a cash buying company, so we have an obvious perspective, but we believe informed sellers make better decisions.

The Estate Agent Route in Eastbourne

How It Works

When you instruct an estate agent in Eastbourne, they will visit your property, provide a valuation, take photographs, and list it on their website and the major property portals. They will conduct viewings, negotiate offers, and manage the sale through to completion. Eastbourne has a good selection of local and national estate agents, with offices on Terminus Road, the Meads area, and throughout the town.

Timeline

The average time from listing to completion in Eastbourne is four to six months. However, the seasonal nature of the coastal market means this can stretch significantly. A property listed in October that does not attract a buyer before the winter slowdown may not generate serious interest until the following March or April, potentially extending the total timeline to eight months or more. This seasonal factor is more pronounced in Eastbourne than in many inland towns.

Costs on a Typical Eastbourne Property

On a property at the Eastbourne average of £290,000, the costs of selling through an estate agent typically include: estate agent commission of £2,900 to £5,800 (1% to 2% plus VAT), solicitor fees of £1,200 to £1,800, Energy Performance Certificate of £60 to £120, and any repairs or cosmetic work needed to make the property presentable. Carrying costs during a six-month sale — mortgage interest, council tax, insurance, and particularly coastal maintenance on period properties — could amount to £4,000 to £7,000. Total costs: approximately £8,000 to £15,000.

Risks

In Eastbourne, the risks of the estate agent route include all the standard ones — chain collapse, mortgage failure, buyer withdrawal — plus some that are specific to the local market. Period properties may throw up survey issues related to damp, outdated wiring, or structural movement. Leasehold flats with short leases may face mortgage restrictions. Retirement properties with age restrictions limit the buyer pool. And the seasonal market pattern means that a failed sale in autumn can leave you stranded until spring.

The Cash Buyer Route in Eastbourne

How It Works

You contact a cash buyer, provide property details, and receive a free valuation and formal cash offer within 24 to 48 hours. If you accept, solicitors are instructed and the conveyancing process begins immediately. No mortgage application, no chain, no seasonal waiting.

Timeline

A typical cash sale in Eastbourne completes in two to four weeks. Some complete in as little as seven days. This timeline is consistent regardless of the time of year — there is no seasonal slowdown with a cash purchase.

Costs

Reputable cash buyers charge no fees and cover the seller's basic legal costs. No estate agent commissions, no EPC charges, and no need to spend money on property preparation. The price offered is the net amount received on completion day.

The Trade-Off: Price

Cash buyers typically offer 75% to 85% of the open market value. On a £290,000 Eastbourne property, that means an offer in the range of £217,500 to £246,500. This discount is the primary trade-off for the speed and certainty provided.

Comparing the Net Outcome

Consider a three-bedroom Victorian house in Old Town valued at £380,000 on the open market.

Estate Agent Route

Sale price: £380,000. Less agent fees (1.5% plus VAT): £6,840. Less solicitor fees: £1,500. Less EPC: £80. Less six months carrying costs: approximately £5,500. Net proceeds: approximately £366,080. Time to completion: four to eight months (accounting for seasonal factors). Risk of sale falling through: approximately 30%.

Cash Buyer Route

Sale price (80% of market value): £304,000. Less fees: £0. Net proceeds: £304,000. Time to completion: two to four weeks. Risk of sale falling through: less than 3%.

The Difference

In this example, the estate agent route delivers approximately £62,080 more. That is a substantial sum, and for sellers with time and a well-maintained property, the traditional route is clearly the better financial option. However, this assumes the sale completes on the first attempt in a reasonable timeframe. Factor in a 30% chance of collapse, potential seasonal delays, and the stress and inconvenience of months of viewings, and the picture becomes more nuanced.

When the Cash Buyer Route Makes More Sense in Eastbourne

Care Home Funding

This is one of the most common scenarios we deal with in Eastbourne. When an elderly homeowner needs to move into residential care, the family home typically needs to be sold to fund care fees. Local authority financial assessments create urgency, and families face the stress of managing a vacant property while their loved one is in care. A cash sale that completes in weeks rather than months can be transformative in these situations.

Leasehold Complications

Eastbourne's large stock of leasehold flats means that short lease issues are a regular problem. A flat with 65 years remaining on its lease may be worth significantly less than the same flat with 125 years, because mortgage lenders restrict lending below 80 years. The cost of a lease extension can run to tens of thousands of pounds. Cash buyers purchase short-lease properties without difficulty, removing this barrier entirely.

Seasonal Timing

If you need to sell your Eastbourne property in autumn or winter, the traditional market is at its weakest. A cash buyer offers the same service year-round, with no seasonal variation in interest or offer levels. For sellers who cannot wait until spring, this consistency is invaluable.

Properties Needing Work

Eastbourne's period properties in Meads, Old Town, and Upperton sometimes need significant investment — rewiring, damp treatment, roof repairs, window replacement — before they appeal to traditional buyers. The coastal environment accelerates deterioration, adding to the cost. Cash buyers purchase in any condition.

Retirement and Sheltered Properties

Selling retirement properties with age restrictions limits the buyer pool dramatically. Add in service charges, communal area costs, and the complexity of management company arrangements, and traditional sales of these properties can be very slow. Cash buyers experienced in this segment handle these transactions as a matter of routine.

When the Estate Agent Route Makes More Sense

No Time Pressure

If you can wait six months or longer and can tolerate the seasonal market pattern, the estate agent route will likely deliver higher net proceeds on a well-maintained property.

Property in Excellent Condition

A beautifully presented property in Meads or Old Town, needing no work and ready to show, is well suited to the traditional market. It will attract interest from buyers relocating from London or Brighton, and may generate competitive offers.

Spring or Summer Listing

If your timing allows you to list in spring, you catch the most active buying season in Eastbourne. The town looks its best, buyer interest is highest, and the chances of a successful sale within a reasonable timeframe are strongest.

Making Your Decision

The choice between a cash buyer and an estate agent in Eastbourne depends on your circumstances. Many homeowners obtain a cash offer alongside an estate agent valuation, giving them a concrete comparison. The cash offer costs nothing and carries no commitment.
