The garden is too much to manage, the stairs are becoming difficult, and the heating bills for a large period house are substantial.</p><p>The desire to move to a smaller flat, a bungalow, or a retirement property is entirely rational. The costs of holding an empty inherited property in Eastbourne, including council tax (potentially at a premium rate for long-term empty properties), insurance, and maintenance, add to the urgency.</p><p>We buy inherited properties in any condition, with contents included if needed. In Eastbourne, where average house prices sit around £290,000, the equity in the property can be substantial. Both parties typically want a clean financial break, and the months-long timeline of a traditional sale can prolong an already painful situation.</p><p>Cash sales provide resolution within weeks. Job loss, illness, business failure, or simply the accumulation of debts can create situations where mortgage payments become unmanageable and the threat of repossession becomes real.</p><p>In these circumstances, time is critical. A traditional sale that takes six months is often too slow — the mortgage lender's legal process may outrun the selling process. A cash sale in as little as two weeks allows homeowners to sell before repossession, clear their mortgage debt, and potentially walk away with equity. We have helped homeowners across Eastbourne sell in time to avoid repossession, and the relief these sellers express is palpable.</p><h2>Relocation</h2><p>Eastbourne's position on the south coast means that it attracts residents from London and the South East, but it also means that when job opportunities arise elsewhere, the need to move can be sudden. A cash sale that completes before the move allows the seller to make a clean break without the stress and cost of managing an empty property from a distance.</p><h2>Landlord Exits</h2><p>Eastbourne's rental market, while smaller than many towns, has a steady landlord community. Changes in tax treatment, increased regulation, and the practical challenges of managing coastal rental properties have driven some landlords to sell. The impact of sea air on properties means higher maintenance costs, while the town's older tenant demographic can present its own management challenges.</p><p>We buy tenanted properties without requiring vacant possession, giving landlords a clean exit without the delay and expense of the eviction process. In Eastbourne, where the seasonal market means that a collapsed sale in autumn can leave you waiting until spring to relist, the impact can be even more severe than in towns with year-round buyer activity.</p><p>Homeowners who come to us after a chain collapse are often understandably reluctant to risk going through the process again.
