3:T22a8,<h2>Living in Eastbourne: The Practical Guide</h2><p>Whether you are considering selling your property in Eastbourne or simply want to understand the local services and infrastructure that affect daily life, this guide covers the three areas that matter most to homeowners: council tax, schools, and transport. These factors directly influence property values and quality of life across the town's neighbourhoods, from the dramatic cliffs of Meads to the modern marina at Sovereign Harbour.</p><h2>Council Tax in Eastbourne</h2><h3>How Council Tax Works</h3><p>Council tax is charged by Eastbourne Borough Council (part of the wider East Sussex County Council structure) to fund local services. Every residential property is placed in a council tax band (A to H) based on its estimated value as at 1 April 1991. The bands still reflect 1991 values, which means that the system can feel disconnected from current property prices — a flat that was worth £60,000 in 1991 but sells for £280,000 today is still banded based on that 1991 figure.</p><h3>Eastbourne Council Tax Bands and Charges</h3><p>Eastbourne's council tax rates include precepts from the Borough Council, East Sussex County Council, Sussex Police and Crime Commissioner, and the East Sussex Fire Authority. The combined charges for the current year range from approximately £1,600 for Band A properties to over £4,800 for Band H.</p><p>The distribution of bands across Eastbourne's neighbourhoods reflects the town's property landscape. Properties in Langney and parts of Hampden Park are predominantly in lower bands (A to C), while homes in Meads and Old Town are more commonly found in the middle to upper bands (D to G). The most prestigious properties in Meads may fall into Band G or H.</p><h3>Council Tax on Empty Properties</h3><p>Eastbourne Borough Council charges full council tax on empty furnished properties. For properties that have been empty and substantially unfurnished for extended periods, a premium may be applied, increasing the council tax charge above the standard rate. This is particularly relevant for homeowners selling their property (if they have already moved out) and executors managing inherited homes. The premium creates a strong financial incentive to sell empty properties quickly.</p><h3>Council Tax Discounts</h3><p>A 25% discount applies if you are the only adult living in the property. Full exemptions may be available in specific circumstances, such as properties undergoing major structural repairs (for up to 12 months), properties left empty by someone who has moved into a care home, and properties that are part of a deceased person's estate (for up to six months after probate is granted). Understanding these exemptions can save money during the selling process, and your solicitor or the council's customer service team can advise on eligibility.</p><h2>Schools in Eastbourne</h2><h3>Overview</h3><p>Eastbourne has a comprehensive network of primary and secondary schools, including community schools, academies, faith schools, and several independent schools. School quality is one of the most significant factors influencing property values at the family home price point, making this a critical consideration for sellers targeting family buyers.</p><h3>Primary Schools</h3><p>Eastbourne has over 20 primary schools spread across its neighbourhoods. Ofsted ratings vary, with a good number rated Good or Outstanding. Schools in Willingdon, Meads, and parts of Hampden Park tend to receive the strongest ratings and attract the most parental interest. The proximity of a property to a well-regarded primary school measurably affects its value, particularly in the family neighbourhoods of Hampden Park, Willingdon, and Polegate.</p><h3>Secondary Schools</h3><p>Secondary education in Eastbourne is provided through several schools including: Ratton School in Willingdon, Cavendish School in central Eastbourne, The Eastbourne Academy in Langney, and Willingdon Community School. Additionally, the town has well-regarded independent schools including Eastbourne College, a co-educational boarding and day school with an excellent reputation, and St Andrew's Prep.</p><p>School performance varies and changes over time. Checking the latest Ofsted reports and exam results is advisable for anyone making property decisions based on school catchments.</p><h3>Further and Higher Education</h3><p>Eastbourne has strong further education provision through Sussex Downs College (now East Sussex College Group), offering A-levels, vocational courses, and adult education. The University of Brighton has a campus in Eastbourne, providing higher education opportunities locally. The presence of the university adds a small student population to the town and supports some rental demand in areas close to the campus.</p><h2>Transport in Eastbourne</h2><h3>Rail Services</h3><p>Eastbourne railway station is the town's primary rail connection and a significant factor in the local property market. The station is on the East Coastway line, providing services in two directions: westward to Lewes (approximately 20 minutes), Brighton (30 to 40 minutes), and connecting services to London Victoria via Lewes and Haywards Heath; and eastward to Hastings (approximately 35 minutes) and beyond.</p><p>Direct services to London Victoria run regularly with a journey time of approximately 90 minutes. For commuters working in London, this makes Eastbourne a viable if lengthy commuting base, and the rail connection supports demand for properties within walking distance of the station, particularly in Upperton and the town centre.</p><p>Hampden Park station, on the same line, provides a secondary rail connection for the eastern part of the town. Polegate station serves the northern area and provides services to Brighton, Lewes, and Eastbourne. The availability of three stations across the wider Eastbourne area is a genuine transport asset.</p><h3>Road Network</h3><p>Eastbourne's road connections are shaped by its coastal position at the end of the A22. The A22 provides the primary route northward, connecting to the A27 at Polegate and continuing towards the M25 and London. The journey to London by road takes approximately 90 minutes to two hours, depending on traffic.</p><p>The A27 connects Eastbourne to Lewes and Brighton to the west and to Pevensey, Bexhill, and Hastings to the east. This is the main east-west route across East Sussex and carries significant traffic. The A259 coast road provides an alternative route along the seafront towards Brighton, passing through Seaford and Newhaven.</p><p>Within Eastbourne, the road network serves the town adequately, though parking can be challenging in central areas and near the seafront, particularly during summer months. Properties with off-street parking command a premium in the town centre and Upperton.</p><h3>Bus Services</h3><p>Stagecoach operates the majority of bus services in the Eastbourne area, connecting all major neighbourhoods to the town centre, the railway station, and the District General Hospital. Routes run from the seafront through the town centre and out to Hampden Park, Langney, Willingdon, and Polegate. Frequencies are generally good on main routes during the day but reduce in the evenings and on Sundays.</p><p>The Coaster service provides a scenic route along the coast to Brighton, while other services connect to Hailsham, Uckfield, and Tunbridge Wells. For a town of Eastbourne's size, the bus network provides reasonable coverage, though car ownership remains important for residents in more suburban areas.</p><h3>Cycling and Walking</h3><p>Eastbourne's flat seafront promenade provides an excellent cycling and walking route stretching from Sovereign Harbour in the east to Holywell in the west. The South Downs Way, one of England's premier long-distance walking routes, begins (or ends) at Beachy Head, providing access to the national park on foot from the western side of the town. The Cuckoo Trail, a traffic-free walking and cycling path, runs from Polegate northward through the East Sussex countryside.</p><p>The terrain across much of Eastbourne is relatively flat, making cycling practical for local journeys. However, the steep inclines up to Meads and towards the Downs present a challenge for less fit cyclists.</p><h2>How These Factors Affect Your Property</h2><p>Council tax, schools, and transport are among the most important practical factors influencing property values and buyer demand across Eastbourne. Properties near good schools, in lower council tax bands, and close to railway stations command premiums.
